The Verifiers

📅 2025-Mar-13 ⬩ ✍️ Ashwin Nanjappa ⬩ 🏷️ book ⬩ 📚 Archive

The Verifiers goes for that (as yet) unique intersection of murder mystery, online dating and the Asian immigrant experience. In this debut novel by Jane Pek we follow the narration of protagonist Claudia Lin, a young New Yorker at her first job at a dating detective agency. A what? This strange business named Veracity handles investigation queries discreetly from online daters. Their client Iris Lettriste wants to know why a guy she was chatting with on a dating app suddenly stopped talking with her. Just when Claudia is biking around town and digging up the dirt on the mystery man, Iris is discovered dead at her apartment. Claudia is asked to drop the case by her bosses Komla Atsina and Becks Rittel, but she continues to pursue it out of her own interest and discovers a much bigger conspiracy at play.

The author brings to bear a deep knowledge of the dating app technology, the harvesting and sale of customer data, how users lie about themselves (the lying limitation) and the use of fake AI bots (synths). What is also great is the Asian immigrant family dynamic, which absorbs a big part of the story, though unfortunately it has nothing to do with the plot. The author nails Claudia’s interactions with her siblings Charlie and Caroline and especially with her never-quite-satisfied mother. Where the novel fails is the murder mystery itself, which is just not interesting enough. I felt like I was plodding through intense dating company business details and Asian family drama in the latter 2 acts, all in the pursuit of a murder which was not meh. There are also too many references to some fictional Inspector Yuan mystery series and to local NYC places, neither of which I could care less about. And finally at the end of a drawn out 3rd act, you finally face the murderer and ask yourself “Really? This is it?”.

Rating: 3/5
