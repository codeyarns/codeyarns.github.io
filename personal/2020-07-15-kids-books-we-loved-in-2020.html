Kids' books we loved in 2020

(First posted on: 2020-07-15T06:56:00.599901-07:00)

This list is solely created from the books we read to our 4-year old son.

Amar Chitra Katha: Panchatantra, Hitopadesha and Jataka Tales

This collection from ACK has 23 comic books. Each book is a compilation of certain types of stories, for example Jackal stories from Panchatantra. I liked that the stories in each book have a common writer and illustrator. These books are meant for older kids, but read out these stories to our kid and he likes looking at the pictures while we do that.

I have great childhood memories of ACK, which I why I got these books for my son. But do note that the societal and cultural themes in several of the stories may not be appropriate in this day and age. Things like caste hierarchy and gender bias pervade the stories involving humans, while the animal stories might have lots of murder and death. It was quite refreshing though to see that people (even upper caste folks) in these stories ate the meat of all types of animals back in the day.

Berenstain Bears

This is a huge series of illustrated short books featuring a bear family. All the stories have a warm and fun vibe and the rhyming prose is a joy to read.
