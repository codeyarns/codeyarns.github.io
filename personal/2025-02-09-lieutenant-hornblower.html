Lieutenant Hornblower is the second book in the Hornblower series by C. S. Forester. At the end of Mr.¬†Midshipman Hornblower he was promoted to junior Lieutenant and that‚Äôs where we find him here on the HMS Renown. He, a new senior lieutenant Bush and the first lieutenant Buckland are positively terrified by their insane captain Sawyer. A mutiny is almost in the plans when the captain falls down stairs and is paralyzed and taken insane. Buckland takes control of the ship and with the help of Bush and some ingenious thinking by Hornblower is able to plan, fight and defeat a Spanish coastal fort in the West Indies and take their ships. The captured Spanish erupt on the Renown, but are put down thanks to timely courageous action by Hornblower. Returning back to safe harbor, they are all tried in court and Hornblower is promoted to Commander of a smaller ship. But just before Hornblower can celebrate, the war with Napoleon ends in truce and all of them lose their jobs. Our protagonist ends up playing card games with naval officers at a club to make ends meet. Will Lady Luck shine on him again and lift him to his naval destiny?

Unlike the first book, which had multiple stories, this book has a single story and I liked that. The narration is from the point of view of Bush, so we get an unique perspective of how Hornblower is viewed by another lieutenant who, though initially wary of his pluck, learns to respect his vitality. The detailed military strategy hatched to capture the Spanish fort and tricks used to convince them to surrender are truly enjoyable to experience. A lot of the writing is also about the hierarchy of power on a ship, the ranks and duties of the officers and their difficulty in getting promoted. We are witness to how good fortune and bad luck can make or break an officer‚Äôs life career. And here is something surprising: I think this work stands out as a great book on engineering management, not kidding. There was quite literally nothing I did not like in this second nail-biting naval adventure and I am eager for the next one.

Rating: 5/5
