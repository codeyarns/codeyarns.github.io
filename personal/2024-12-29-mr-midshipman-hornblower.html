Mr. Midshipman Hornblower

📅 2024-Dec-29 ⬩ ✍️ Ashwin Nanjappa ⬩ 🏷️ book ⬩ 📚 Archive

I recently learnt that there was a Hornblower series of naval adventures that is similar to my favorite Aubrey-Maturin novels. And that led me to start with the first book Mr. Midshipman Hornblower. Both the series are centered around characters on British naval fighting ships during the Napoleonic Wars.

In this first book, we watch Hornblower start his career as a midshipman (entry level in officer rank) and get promoted to Lieutenant by the end. Though he is tormented by a terrible bully on his first ship, he snags a transfer to the Indefatigable under Captain Pellew that kick starts his career. Each of the several chapters in the book deal with separate adventures he is involved in while the ship scours the coasts of France and Spain, trying to pick off enemy merchant ships or fight their naval vessels. Towards the middle of the book, Spain aligns with France and that leaves Britain with just the single port of Gibraltor along that coastline. Life thrusts Hornblower into increasing levels of responsibility, independence and danger through the chapters. Though barely 20, his feet are put to the fire and he is forced to pick and choose between the lives of the men under him. He is able to use his inexperience to devise innovative strategies to win battles for his captain. Through inexperience and bad timing, a few of his tasks fail and in the end he is captured and imprisoned by Spain. The novel ends on a happy note, when through his bravado in saving innocent Spanish lives, he is freed and the British promote him to Lieutenant.

This series written by C. S. Forester was a great discovery for me. I found this first novel quite riveting, finding parallels to Hornblower’s journey in my own fatherhood and career. A single arc might have resulted in a stronger novel, but the singular chapters are all interesting too on their own. After a long while, I was looking up sailing-ship jargon, living on a ship and getting lost in the history and places of the Napoleonic era.

Rating: 4/5

ISBN: 9780316289122
