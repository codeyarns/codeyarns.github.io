I felt refreshed returning to a murder mystery, Gone for Good in our book club. Written by Joanna Schaffhausen, this is the first in her Detective Annalisa Vega series. We start off with our protagonist, Chicago PD detective Annalisa Vega, assigned to a murder that seems to be a copycat of 7 serial murders committed 20 years ago by the Lovelorn killer. His macabre signature was to tie up his female victims in the style of shibari and suffocate them to death. We soon discover why the killer came out of the shadows after 20 long years - his latest victim Grace Harper had been a part of a group of amateur detectives called Grave Diggers and she had been close to revealing his identity. This serial killer is not a stranger to Vega too, since he had killed her neighbor Kathy Duffy, whose family had been close to her. As the killer starts to get fatally close to Vega, she is drawn between her two ex partners.

This book was engrossing and easy to finish in just two sittings. I loved the setup, the settings in Chicago and the mystery of this serial killer. Vega and her two lovers felt truly thin and out of place for this plot. The chapters ping pong between the story and journal entries by Grace Harper, this adds an extra point of view to the telling. The third act was truly weak, with the ludicrous reveal of the killer and the harebrained climax caper. It is somewhat salvaged by a truly surprising twist at the end that shatters Vegaâ€™s family. Apparently the books get better in this series, so I might be tempted to try a Vega mystery again.

Rating: 4/5
