It is no secret that I love the murder mysteries by Keigo Higashino. Malice is the first novel in his Police Detective Kaga series and the English translation is by Alexander O. Smith. Set in 1996, the plot opens with the mysterious murder of famous author Hidaka that is discovered by his new second wife Rie and his close friend Osamu Nonoguchi. In this book, there is also the added wrinkle of why - the motive which dogs Kaga, why was Hidaka killed? We learn that it was due to malice, but the details elude us until the final chapter.

The book is nail-biting, as always, and it took me just 2 sittings to finish it. My mind was engrossed in the multiple possibilities and motives until late in the night. I also liked the unique writing style of this particular book, as either journal entries or police reports, that really made me think harder. This is yet another perfect un-put-downable Higashino book, the only minor quibble being a bit too much time spent on the motive.

Rating: 4/5
