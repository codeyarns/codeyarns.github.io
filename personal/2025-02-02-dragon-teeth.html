Dragon Teeth

📅 2025-Feb-02 ⬩ ✍️ Ashwin Nanjappa ⬩ 🏷️ book ⬩ 📚 Archive

Dragon Teeth is one of those posthumously published novels of Michael Crichton. Set in 1875, this story thrusts Yale University student William Johnson into the rivalry between paleontologists Marsh and Cope. Johnson signs on to Marsh’s expedition for dinosaur bones and starts off on a long railroad journey from Philadelphia to the American West (Wyoming, Idaho and Montana). He soon learns that Marsh is an evil smooth-talking cut-throat and ends up joining Cope’s expedition. Cope and his ragtag bunch of explorers are thrust into the midst of a war between the US Army and Native American tribes. They venture far into Native American territory on horseback and discover amazing new kinds of dinosaurs, even when thwarted by Marsh’s henchmen. On the return, Johnson is separated with the precious boxes of bones and he endures a year long story of survival in dangerous Wild West towns filled with insane trigger-happy shooters.

Apparently the story is based on a real rivalry between the professors of that era. It is an easy read, with the emphasis being on surviving in the great outdoors and life in Wild West towns and very little about the bones. The plot itself is a bit of a stretch, relying on innumerable contrivances to enable Johnson to survive and hold on to the bones. Similar to Pirate Latitudes, it is clear why a perfectionist like Crichton did not end up publishing this during his lifetime. Though it is entertaining, it does not have the depth or polish of his famous works.

Rating: 3/5
