A world controlled by nerds where AI and automation has eliminated almost all jobs. Sounds scarily like the future of AI? This is actually the setting of Player Piano, the 1952 debut novel by acclaimed author Kurt Vonnegut. Engineers and managers played a prominent role in a 3rd Industrial Revolution and a 3rd World War and now they are in charge. In cities like New York, the engineers and managers live in high-tech mansions in a fenced Ilium Works conclave, while plebians live in Homestead. People who are either not smart enough or interested to be engineers can work in the Army or the Reeks and Wrecks (Reconstruction and Reclamation Corps). The engineers think the normal people, though mostly jobless, should be content since they have a basic income that covers their health and all modern amenities. However, the talented people who lost their jobs to machines are frustrated and a Ghost Shirt Society pulls them together to foment a secret uprising across the US.

We follow the life of our protagonist Dr.Â Paul Proteus, the leader of Ilium as he navigates his career and relationship, and fantasizes throwing it all away in pursuit of being free like his friend Finnerty. Being the son of a world famous scientist, his career is about to be boosted, if he toes the company line, as his too-perfect wife Anita keeps reminding him. But Paul has been getting drawn to the dirty and real life in Homestead and the talented-but-jobless people out there. Things come to a head at the company retreat where Paul is recruited to infiltrate the Ghost Shirts. However, to the shock of his bosses and his wife, Paul actually sympathizes with the people and they make him his leader and start an uprising against the machines.
