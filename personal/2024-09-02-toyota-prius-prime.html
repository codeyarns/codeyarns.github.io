We recently got a Toyota Prius Prime, 2024 XSE edition, for our family. This post is an attempt to note the various features, jargon and technologies associated with this vehicle.

Regular features

  • Driving Modes: Different driving modes can be chosen using the Drive Mode select switch by the driver‚Äôs right side. Supported modes are Normal, Eco, Sport and Custom.

  • Parking brake (manual): Pull the (P) switch to set it. Press the (P) switch or press accelerator pedal to release the brake.

  • Parking brake (automatic): In automatic mode, the car sets the parking brake automatically when shifted to P. To turn ON this mode, pull and hold the (P) switch until a buzzer sounds. To turn OFF this mode, keep brake pressed and press down on the (P) switch until a buzzer sounds.

Driving assist features

  • Lane Change Assist (LCA)

  • Lane Tracing Assist (LTA)

  • Lane Departure Alert (LDA)

  • Proactive Driving Assist (PDA)

  • Front Cross Traffic Alert (FCTA)

  • Road Sign Assist (RSA)

  • Dynamic Radar Cruise Control (DRCC)

  • Driver Monitor Camera (DMC)

  • Traffic Jam Assist (TJA)

  • Blind Spot Monitor (BSM): Indicates to the driver if there are vehicles in the blind spot and it is not safe to change lanes. The driver is informed by illuminating the indicators on the outside rear view mirrors and a buzzing sound. This feature uses radar sensors installed on the left/right corners of the rear bumper.

  • Lane Departure Alert (LDA)

  • Safe Exit Assist (SEA): Indicates to the driver if there are vehicles in the blind spot and it is not safe to open the door. When the driver opens a door, they are informed by illuminating the indicators on the outside rear view mirrors and a buzzing sound. This feature uses radar sensors installed on the left/right corners of the rear bumper.

  • Intuitive Parking Assist (IPA): Shows the proximity of wall/objects to the car when parking the car. This is displayed on the multimedia display and also with a buzzing sound. This feature uses sensors on the front corners and rear corners.

  • Rear Cross Traffic Alert (RCTA): When backing out of a parking spot, this uses the rear camera display to warn if there are vehicles crossing from the sides at the back of the car. This feature uses the rear side sensors.

  • Rear Camera Detection (RCD): Detects pedestrians right behind the vehicle. Sounds a buzzer and displays on multimedia display. This feature uses the rear camera (not the sensors).

  • ParKing Support Brake (PKSB): My car does not have this feature.

  • Toyota Teammate Advanced Park: My car does not have this feature.

  • Pre-Collision System (PCS)

  • Electronically Controlled Brake (ECB)

  • Anti-lock Brake System (ABS)

  • Brake assist

  • Vehicle Stability Control (VSC)

  • Enhanced VSC (EVSC)

  • Traction Control (TRAC)

  • Active Cornering Assist (ACA)

  • Hill-start Assist Control

  • Electric Power Steering (EPS)

  • Secondary Collision Brake

Incident 1 (Oct 2024)

Day 1

I could not avoid hitting a fist-sized stone with my front-left tire while accelerating to freeway speed at the end of the on-ramp. The multi-information display immediately sounded and displayed a huge warning of extremely low pressure in that tire. While driving a bit more to assess the situation, I noticed that every half a mile the tire had the feel of going over something. I slowly exited the freeway with the hazard lights ON and parked in the nearest parking lot.

I called Toyota Roadside Assistance since the car was still under the Toyota Plus coverage. After providing the VIN number, which is displayed on the front-right outside, a tow truck was assigned to me. I noted that Toyota Roadside Assistance outsources to AAA which assigned a tow truck from Fortes Brothers. Though I was given an initial wait time of 50 minutes, the truck only arrived 1.5 hours later!

It was a wheel lift tow truck. The guy drove the car over some pads and then lifted the front wheels up using the truck lift. I sat in the truck and rode to America‚Äôs Tire.

The tow truck guy put down the car in the America‚Äôs Tire parking lot and left. The tire person assessed the car and said that a new tire might be needed. They didn‚Äôt have the tire, but could get it next day. So I left the car there overnight.

Day 2

Next day noon I got a text that the tire was replaced. When I went there though, the tire guy informed me that the dashboard was fully lit up with all possible error codes. I could see in my Toyota phone app that there were 10 errors, all of them serious. The tire guy scared me that maybe the car was not put in the right mode when it was towed leading to failure of the transmission system and cooking the car! I was shocked and called Toyota Roadside to ask for advice. They recommended getting the car towed to my dealership for an assessment. I booked a tow with Toyota Roadside Assistance, and just to be sure asked for a flatbed tow truck to keep all 4 wheels off the road. The tow truck did not arrive even after 3 hours and I eventually canceled it since the tire shop was closing.

Day 3

I called Toyota Roadside Assistance again and booked a flatbed tow truck. Just to confirm, I called the Fortes Brothers directly and they informed me (differently) that flatbeds are super hard to get and their truck would have a dolly or skates to put under all 4 wheels and that would be enough. By this time all the errors had disappeared on my car and just the check engine warning was on. The car was towed to the Toyota dealership and after I explained the situation, they decided to first do a diagnostic check. I also requested for a reimbursement of the fees since the problem was created by a tow truck provided by Toyota Roadside themselves. I was informed after a few hours that the car was ready for pickup. They were able to find all the error codes in the logs, but after resetting the system and driving it around no errors showed up.

Root cause

So what caused the errors? After reading this post and thinking about the situation, I think the culprit is the automatic parking brake. I have it enabled it on my car and my guess is that the first tow truck guy could not figure out how to disable this when towing. And the sensors must have freaked out that the rear wheels were rotating when in parking mode.
