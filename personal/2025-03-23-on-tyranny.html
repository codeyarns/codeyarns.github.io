On Tyranny is a tiny book written by Timothy Snyder containing 20 recommendations for citizens to face up to tyranny. The word tyranny can mean different things to different people, but this book is focused on facing up to â€œthe usurpation of power by a single individual or group, or the circumvention of law by rulers for their own benefitâ€. Published in 2017 and seemingly updated a few years later, it is actually focused on what is happening politically in the US at this moment.

The author seems to have expertise in the 1900-1950s rise of communism and fascism in Europe, how Germans let Hitler rise in power and stood by watching as he grabbed all power and committed atrocities on Jews. So, he applies learnings from that part of the world history to present-day USA and offers 20 succinct recommendations. The book though has blinders - it does not recognize that dictators or fascists do not rise in power in a vacuum and that they become an attractive option to people who have genuine problems in life. So what to do after a tyranny is prevented - unless a strong alternate solution is visible, the grieving people are unlikely to be moved.

I liked how dense and distilled the book reads, with every sentence terse and loaded with gravity. The book is of course a call to action and with its tiny size is a quick read. Being the size of a pocket book, it is also an easy book to carry around or gift to people.

Rating: 4/5

The 20 lessons:

  1. Do not obey in advance.
  2. Defend institutions.
  3. Beware the one-party state.
  4. Take responsibility for the face of the world.
  5. Remember professional ethics.
  6. Be wary of paramilitaries.
  7. Be reflective if you must be armed.
  8. Stand out.
  9. Be kind to our language.
  10. Believe in truth.

  13. Practice corporeal politics.
  14. Establish a private life.
  15. Contribute to good causes.
  16. Learn from peers in other countries.
  17. Listen for dangerous words.
  18. Be calm when the unthinkable arrives.
  19. Be a patriot.
  20. Be as courageous as you can.
