One Piece: East Blue

📅 2023-Dec-11 ⬩ ✍️ Ashwin Nanjappa ⬩ 🏷️ book ⬩ 📚 Archive

The appearance of the live action One Piece TV series on Netflix spurred me to read a manga after a really long time. I picked up the tome One Piece: East Blue, which is a compilation of the first 3 volumes of the manga. Created by Eiichiro Oda at a surprisingly young age of 22, this manga has turned to be the best selling manga series of all time.

The story is a set in a different fantasy world and time, where the land is surrounded by various oceans and pirates rule the seas. Not all pirates are bad and our hero Monkey D. Luffy aspires to be the king of (good) pirates and to find the legendary One Piece treasure. He gains the superpower of super-stretching after eating the magical Devil Fruit of the gum-gum tree in his childhood. And once he is older, he is off on the seas to gather a motley set of characters to form his crew. These adventures and the fights with potential villains keeps the story chugging along.

I read the English version of East Blue published by Viz Media. This version reads just like the original manga, i.e., back-to-front with helpful directions for readers new to manga on how to read it. This tome has the first 3 volumes: Romance Dawn, Buggy the Clown and Don’t Get Fooled Again. The unique setting of this world, the characters and the non-stop sarcastic humor are top notch. The drawing is good and the action panels would satisfy the younger crowd I would assume. The manga runs fast and once you are invested in the story, it is easy to finish the 600+ pages easily. However, I do not think I would read the series since I am bit too old for the shonen level slapstick gags and will only be looking at seinen manga in the future.

Rating: 3/4
