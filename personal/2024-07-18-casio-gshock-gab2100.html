I recently discovered that Casio had lots of new watches now that could run forever (using solar power) and also synchronize time using Bluetooth to a smartphone. This fit my preference for a wristwatch that I can just pick and wear and it always shows the correct time and is always charged. So I had to get one and I chose the Casio G-Shock GB-B2100, more accurately the GAB2100CY-1A which has yellow hands.

  • Analog hands: They are big and bright yellow and easily readable. They spin themselves to the right time when the time is set using the phone app.

  • Digital display: This is a 2-line display with seconds on the top line and date/day/time and other information displayed on the bottom line. In the model I got, this is a grey-on-black display and it truly sucks! It is barely readable even in sunlight and impossible to read in the low light. Casio is introducing a lot of watches with this kind of display and I would avoid them and choose the traditional black-on-grey display.

  • Light: The light is bright and is located in the bottom-right corner. I have had to resort to using it to read the digital display, the display is truly that bad!

  • Solar charging: The dial of this watch has a transparent solar panel and charges itself.

  • Bluetooth: Install the Casio Watches app on the phone to communicate with it using Bluetooth.

  • Casio Watches: This is Casioâ€™s smartphone app for both Android and iOS. I like that pretty much all the settings of the watch can be done right from this app itself. Supported features include setting alarms, setting timers, setting reminders, setting the world time zone, automatic time synchronization with the phone and a phone finder.
