The Princess Diaries

A high school girl Mia (Anne Hathaway) in San Francisco discovers that she is actually a princess and heir to the throne of a small (fictional) European nation called Genovia. Her queen grandmother Clarisse (Julie Andrews) arrives in SF with her bodyguard Joe to help Anne through her high-school troubles and help her makeover into a princess. The only reason it is even watchable are the two principal women. Watched it on DVD.

Rating: 3/5

Premium Rush

There is no looking away when incredible bike messenger Wilee (Joseph Gordon-Levitt) is racing amidst Manhattan traffic on his fixed-gear no-brakes bike to deliver messages. The entire movie is about one delivery of his that turns out involving Chinese gangs, a corrupt cop and holds innocent lives in the balance. Some of the insane scenes looked artificial, but for the most part this is an adrenaline-pumping bike chase from start to finish. Got to wonder how they shot so many carefully choreographed heavy-traffic scenes on so many roads in Manhattan.

Rating: 4/5
