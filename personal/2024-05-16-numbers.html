I typically like to binge on a single TV series at a time, watching 1 episode each day. So, what I usually look for are older TV series that have a lot of seasons and episodes. I am happy to say that I was well entertained by the entire 6 seasons (2005-2010) of the Numbers (Numb3rs) TV series on Amazon Prime.

In every episode, we see FBI agent Don Eppes and his team try to solve a new crime mystery that happens in and around LA. To crack the puzzle, his math genius brother Charles Eppes and his physicist mentor Larry Fleinhardt ponder and apply a mathematical method or a computer algorithm. They are also helped by Charlesâ€™ fiancee Amita and their father Alan Eppes.

This was an easy series to like and watch every day. Charles would introduce the math concept with a real world example (to explain to the FBI agents) and it would be displayed with a visualization in every episode. Though the math or compsci solution is tried and applied at impossibly fast speeds, they all had a basis in truth and I never felt that they were insulting intelligence. Especially with machine learning and computer algorithms, I could see that all their approaches were somewhat plausible. Not surprisingly, the show runners apparently consulted with mathematicians on every single episode. If you are looking for a fun crime series that does not insult your intelligence, this is a good pick.

Rating: 4/5
