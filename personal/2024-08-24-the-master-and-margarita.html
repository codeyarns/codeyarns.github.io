Having not read any Russian classic before I jumped on the chance to read The Master and Margarita when it came along. This book written by Mikhail Bulgakov appears highly recommended among Russian works. I read the edition translated by Richard Pevear and Larissa Volokhonsky and apparently it is a decent one.

The story begins in Moscow with the appearance of the devil Woland and his entourage which includes Koroviev, Azazello, Hella and a black cat named Behemoth. Woland informs an editor Berlioz the exact manner of how he would die in an accident in a short while. That tragic death of Berlioz witnessed by his minion Ivan Homeless and the subsequent acts of Woland and his gang in the city create the stage for an incredible showpiece of dark comedy set amidst the Soviet system.
