Rosicrucian Egyptian Museum

This is a museum in San Jose that has lots of original artifacts and replicas from ancient Egypt. The museum building has the facade of a huge Egpytian temple and is nestled amidst trees, fountains, fish ponds and small gardens. For an entrance fee of $10, there is easily 2 hours worth of soaking oneself in Egyptian history and culture here. There are a shocking number of original artifacts on display here, including mummified bodies, coffins, tablets, writing, footwear, ornaments, papyrus and statues. Plus they have replicas of all the important artifacts (like King Tutâ€™s sacrophagus) making the visit a vivid experience. There is a cozy room with settings for many visitors to sit down and play the ancient Egyptian board game Senet. Kids will love this, the stamps they can collect all over the halls, the AI animatropic puppet that answers their questions and the Koi fish pond outside.

Rating: 5/5
