I read Sphere a second time in 2024 and was surprised to see that I had forgotten everything from my first read in 2006. Psychologist Norman Johnson is whisked away by the Navy to the middle of the Pacific ocean. He is taken down to an underwater habitat to join a team of scientists and Navy people and study a crashed craft. To their surprise, they discover that it is a huge alien spacecraft and find a perfect black sphere inside it. While they try to unravel the mystery of the craft and the sphere, dangerous creatures attack their habitat and start killing them one by one.

As always Michael Crichton is super interesting to read when he sets up his techno premise. The intriguing idea in the first act is that the craft is actually from Earthâ€™s future, sent to study a black hole where it might have picked up the alien sphere and traveled back in time to our planet. The plot plods along in the second act while they figure out how to communicate with the alient Jerry entity and it starts to send scary sea creatures to attack them. The final act has some great twists, as we suspect whether it is the subconscious of Harry (mathematician), Beth (zoologist) or Norman which is playing tricks on them. In the Hollywood-like climax, the 3 survivors escape destroying the sphere and lose their memory of the adventure.

Though I enjoyed it thoroughly in my first read, I found it a bit disappointing in the second read. The craft and the sphere are surprisingly short on any exciting technology reveals. Even the team itself feels more artificially put together compared to the team in Jurassic Park. The action or excitement does not build up until the climax. There are some good science bits, like Tedâ€™s explanation of space-time using a fruit bowl. The idea of the sphere giving the humans the power to manifest their imagination was pretty cool too. And the final act really proves the book to be a great psychological thriller.

Rating: 4/5
