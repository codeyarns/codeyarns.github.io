Kids books we loved in 2025

📅 2025-Feb-03 ⬩ ✍️ Ashwin Nanjappa ⬩ 🏷️ book ⬩ 📚 Archive

This is a collection of books that our kids loved to read this year.

Books loved by 9-year old

  • Guts (by Raina Telgemeier): Every graphic novel by this author is based on her personal life and it really shows in the kinds of detail she depicts. This one is quite mature, looking at how the relationship of the author evolved with her sister as their new brother joined their family.

  • Out of my Dreams (by Sharon M. Draper): Another followup to the Out of my Mind that my son read with rapt interest.

  • Out of my Heart (by Sharon M. Draper): Final book in the Out of my Mind series.

Books loved by 4-year old

  • And to Think That I Saw It On Mulberry Street (by Dr. Seuss): Apparently the very first book by Dr. Seuss. Incredible to imagine that he knocked it out of the park in the very first one. All his familiar strokes are already in his first book: inventive new words, rhyming story and great art.

  • Dr. Seuss’s Sleep Book (by Dr. Seuss): Incredible inventive sleeptime rhyming poem.

  • The Lorax (by Dr. Seuss): Lorax, thneed and other amazing words in this pertinent story about nature and human greed.

  • The Sneetches And Other Stories (by Dr. Seuss): Many lovely bedtimes reading the funny rhyming stories to my girl.

  • Dr. Seuss’s Horse Museum (by Dr. Seuss): An introduction to the various forms of art by using the horse as an example. As an adult, even I found this to be entertaining and educational.

  • Robot Dreams (by Sara Varon): An entire graphic novel with no words about the friendship and separation of a dog and a robot. My daughter strangely loved it, but I felt really moved by the book myself. There are so many of life’s adult moments that the author portrays poignantly with no pomp.
