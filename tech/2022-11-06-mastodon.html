I chose to try Mastodon after searching for an alternative platform to migrate to after quitting Twitter. Here are some notes I have taken about it during my first few days with Mastodon:

  • Mastodon is a micro-blogging platform, similar to Twitter. Unlike Twitter, it is federated, so you have an account on a server and posts are shared between all the Mastodon servers in the world.

  • Post: You publish or post short updates of up to 500 characters that are called toots (like tweets).

  • Instance (aka server): Unlike Twitter, there is no central provider. It is a decentralized network of Mastodon servers, each hosting posts of their own set of users.To get started, you sign up on any Mastodon server to get an account. These servers are also called instances. Think of it like email accounts on email providers.

  • Username: Mastodon username is of the form @your-username@your-instance. For example: @codeyarns@mastodon.social. To mention on your own instance, you could use the shorthand @username.

  • Profile:Your profile and your posts will appear at your instance at https://your-instance/@your-username. For example: https://mastodon.social/@codeyarns. Your profile information is also available in JSON at https://your-instance/@your-username.json. For example: https://mastodon.social/@codeyarns.json

  • Follow: You can follow any other Mastodon user who is on any instance. This typically means clicking their Follow button. Some instances might require you to specify your username (in the form @your-username@your-instance) to do this. Some instances might require you to copy that user‚Äôs URL or username and paste it on your instance‚Äôs search bar to add that user. (I know, this is irritating.)

  • Reply (‚Ü©Ô∏é): You can reply to any toot and all replies are at the same level and in chronological order.

  • Boost (‚áå): This is similar to retweet in Twitter. The post appears in the feed of all your followers.

  • Favourite (‚≠ê): Add post to your Favourites list, notifies the author of the post and increments the Favourite count on that post. I see this as equivalent to a Like (üëç) on Facebook.

  • Bookmark (üîñ): Adds the post to your Bookmarks list and does nothing else. Bookmarks are private to you and no one else can see them.

  • Mute a follower: They can still follow you, but you won‚Äôt see their posts in your feed.

  • Block a follower: Cut the follow ties completely.

  • Privacy: Every post you create can have privacy of one of the following:

    • Public (üåê)
    • Unlisted: Same as public, but not shown in Explore or Local timeline.
    • Followers-only (üîí): Visible to your followers and anyone mentioned in the post.
    • Private/Mentioned (@): Only visible to folks mentioned in the post.

  • Tagged posts:

    • All the posts with a tag can be seen at https://<your-server>/tags/<your-tag>. For example: https://mastodon.social/tags/TheEconomist
    • All the tagged posts of an user can be seen at https://<your-server>/@<your-username>/tagged/<your-tag>. For example: https://mastodon.social/@codeyarns/tagged/TheEconomist

  • Filters: Create and manage your filters at https://<your-server>/filters. For example: https://mastodon.social/filters I find them useful to hide posts in my feed that match certain keywords/tags.

  • Live feeds: These feeds show a chronological timeline of posts.

    • This server (aka Local): shows the latest posts from the users of your server. For example: https://mastodon.social/public/local
    • Other servers: shows the latest posts from other servers that your instance follows. For example: https://mastodon.social/public/remote
    • All (aka Federated): shows the latest posts from all accounts on all instances that are followed by your instance. For example: https://mastodon.social/public

  • Explore timeline: shows the currently popular items from across the the fediverse.

    • Popular posts: https://mastodon.social/explore
    • Popular tags: https://mastodon.social/explore/tags
    • Popular people: https://mastodon.social/explore/suggestions
    • Popular links: https://mastodon.social/explore/links

  • Keyboard shortcuts supported by your instance are at https://<your-server>/web/keyboard-shortcuts. For example: https://mastodon.social/keyboard-shortcuts

  • Rules of an instance: Each instance can have their own rules which can be found at about/more of that domain. For example: https://mastodon.social/about/more

  • RSS feed of my posts will be available at https://<your-server>/users/<your-username>.rss or https://<your-server>/@<your-username>.rss for easy reading of posts using RSS readers. For example: https://mastodon.social/@codeyarns.rss

  • Search:

    • Add from:me to search only your own posts.
    • Add in:library to search your own posts and those that you interacted with.
    • Add from:username to search posts by a specific user.

Usage

  • Migration: It is easy to move your account to a different server by following this guide. Your followers will be automatically pointed to your new account and visitors to your old profile will be forwarded too. Your posts on the old server cannot be moved, but they will still exist as long as the old server is not dead.

  • Bridging your Mastodon account into Bluesky: Thanks to the magic of Bridgy Fed, posts from your Mastodon account can be bridged across to Bluesky. Login into Mastodon with your Mastodon account, for example @codeyarns@mastodon.social. Find and follow the Mastodon account of Bridgy Fed for Bluesky. In a short while, you will receive a private reply from this account that mentions that a Bluesky account that bridges your Mastodon account has been created. For example, @codeyarns.mastodon.social.ap.brid.gy. The fed.brid.gy internal status page for this bridged account can be seen here. All the posts from your Mastodon account will now appear at Bluesky as posted by the above bridged Bluesky clone account of yours. Bluesky users might find your bridged Mastodon account and follow it too.

  • Unbridging your Mastodon account into Bluesky: My bridge account on Bluesky was cross-posting my Mastodon posts, however I could not get notified when someone replied to those posts on Bluesky. So, in the end I decided to tear down the bridge. To do this, unfollow and block the Bridgy Fed for Bluesky account on Mastodon.

  • Bridging your Bluesky account into Mastodon: Thanks to the magic of fed.brid.gy, Bluesky can be bridged to Mastodon (or generally Fediverse). Login into your Bluesky account, for example @codeyarns.bsky.social. Find and follow the Bluesky account @ap.brid.gy. Your Bluesky account should appear in the fediverse as @codeyarns.bsky.social@bsky.brid.gy. These are the instructions, but it never worked for me and I just gave up.
