Problem

Deciding to educate myself, I recently picked up a 10-DVD box set of Star Trek: The Original Series (Season 1). I plopped the first DVD into a Sony DVP-SR510H player that was connected over HDMI to a Hisense 55A6H TV running Google TV. The remastered video and audio sounded great, but the DVD did not seem to have English subtitles. It only had French and Spanish subtitles and when chosen those were displayed correctly.

For many years now, I have relied on English subtitles when watching TV series and movies. It seemed surprising that the release of such a popular TV series would be missing English subtitles.

Solution

Looking at the back of the box, it had the logos for CC (Closed Captions) and NTSC. I already knew that CC was information transmitted along with the analog TV signals. And since Star Trek was a TV series, it made sense that the DVD creators decided to retain the original CC info transmitted over NTSC video in the discs. How to get this CC info out from the DVD and display on the TV?

Checking online, I found that this CC info cannot be transmitted over HDMI. So, I hooked up the 3-color component video cable that has yellow (for video) and red-white (for right-left audio) to the DVD player and the TV. In the TV inputs, I chose AV and I knew I was getting NTSC video since it was noticeably lower quality than HDMI video. However, no CC info was displayed.

Next, I dug into the Google TV settings for CC and enabled all of it. This is Settings -> Accessibility -> Captions. Still no CC info!

Finally, I suspected that the DVD player was messing with the raw analog output before sending it to the TV. So, I dove into its screen setup -> Mode (Progressive) and changed from Auto to Video.

Finally, I was able to see the original English version of Closed Captions on the Star Trek video!

PS: The same trick did not work on my portable Sony DVP-FX950 DVD player. Probably because it is only sending digital video to its internal display and does not have any option to use the analog video info.
