BlueSky is an online service similar to Twitter to read, repost and share small posts.

  • Signup: Sign up for an account at bsky.app. Choose Bluesky as the service and your Bluesky ID will be @codeyarns.bluesky.social. If you are able to provide a custom service provider, then your Bluesky ID will be @codeyarns.<something>.

  • Online webpage: Once signed up, you can read, repost and share posts online at bsky.app/profile/codeyarns.bsky.social.

  • Follow: The accounts you follow will appear as following on your profile. The accounts that follow you will appear as followers on your profile.

  • Feeds: Posts from your following accounts will appear in the default Following feed. You can also find and add other custom feeds like Followers (posts of your followers), Mutuals (posts from mutually following accounts), Mentions (only posts that mention you).

  • Lists: You can add accounts to one or more lists you create at bsky.app/lists. Even accounts that you do not follow can be added to lists. I find this a handy way to categorize my following into lists and not have their posts appear in the main Following feed.

  • Posts: Posts have a 300 character limit and they cannot be edited once posted.

  • Tags: Posts can have hashtags. Posts of a specific hashtag can be viewed at bsky.app/hashtag/TheEconomist.

  • Bridging: For details on how to bridge from Bluesky to Mastodon or from Mastodon to Bluesky, see my Mastodon post.
