Tech links of January 2025

📅 2025-Jan-02 ⬩ ✍️ Ashwin Nanjappa ⬩ 🏷️ linkblog ⬩ 📚 Archive

  • Death and Life in Silicon Valley: Erik Lindholm’s retirement talk about his 35-year career at SGI and NVIDIA. Covers his early personal life, work in Japan, SGI and move to NVIDIA. Creation of T&L unit in GPU (instead of CPU), launch of GeForce 256 (Geometry Force) GPU, vertex/pixel shaders, unified shaders, warp to work on packed 16 vertices/pixels and road to SIMT/CUDA. Highly recommended watch for graphics and CUDA enthusiasts.

  • Is XYplorer really written in VB6?: Short history of Visual Basic, why XYplorer is written (and successful) in it to this day and some of its real limitations.

  • Stop Trying To Schedule A Call With Me: Humorous telling of the journey of a typical relationship between an engineer and the startup whose product he tries out to solve a problem. I have been on both sides of this relationship, so I know it’s true.

  • Furnace - How to Relight a Pilot Light: This video shows the pilot light and thermocouple in a gas furnace used for heating homes in the US and how they work.

  • How A Gas Furnace Works: Animated schematic of how a gas furnace works at home.
