Ubuntu: Mount NTFS Partitions at Boot

2010-04-01 15:20:03+00:00

Since I am dual-booting the laptop between Windows XP and Ubuntu, I would like to access my NTFS partitions in Ubuntu for both read and write. To tell Ubuntu to mount them everytime at boot:

  • Install the ntfs-config package. It ships with the ntfs-3g driver which has safe write capability for NTFS.

  • From the terminal invoke:

    $ sudo ntfs-config

In the ntfs-config dialog, choose the NTFS partitions you want mounted at boot and choose Write capability for internal drives. ntfs-config mounts the partitions immediately and also adds the relevant mount entries to /etc/fstab so they are also mounted at boot everytime.
