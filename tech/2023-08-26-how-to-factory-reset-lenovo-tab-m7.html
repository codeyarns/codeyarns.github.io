You may need to factory reset the Lenovo Tab M7 (Gen3) if you run into irrecoverable issues. Here are some ways that might work based on my experience:

Reset using external buttons

The steps described here worked for me:

  • First power off the tablet.
  • Now keep the Volume Up, Volume Down and Power - all 3 of them pressed down until you see the Lenovo logo appear on the display. This might take 30 seconds or more.
  • When the Lenovo logo appears, only let go of the Power button and continue to keep the Volume Up and Volume Down buttons pressed.
  • After a few seconds, the Android Recovery menu with a list of options will appear! Let go of the Volume buttons now.
  • Move up and down the items using the Volume buttons. When you come to the Wipe data/factory reset option, press the Power button to choose that. Confirm and the phone will be wiped.
  • After that choose the Reboot system now option. You will reboot into a fresh tablet and will need to set up the tablet from scratch.

What does not work

  • There is no way to factory reset if the device is already booted into Android and you are inside a non-admin account. You need to use the physical buttons method above.

  • Keeping Volume Down and Power buttons pressed takes the tablet into a FASTBOOT mode. Not sure what I can do after that.

  • Keeping Volume Up and Power buttons pressed takes the tablet into a META mode. Not sure what I can do after that.
