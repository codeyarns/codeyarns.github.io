External displays

  • Dell C3422WE: This is a curved display with an integrated USB hub. It was straightforward to connect this display to the MBP using the display’s USB-C cable. The display was usable as an extended display when the MBP lid was open. The display was also usable as the sole display when the MBP lid was closed.

  • Dell WD19TBS, P3424WEB and U2415: In this setup, two displays (P3424WEB and U2415) were connected to the WD19TBS dock using DisplayPort and HDMI respectively, and the USB-C cable of the dock was plugged into MBP. In this mode, MBP could recognize only the U2415 and mirrored the content on the P3424WEB. Apparently, MBP supports 2 external displays in addition to its own display, but it requires at least one of those to be connected using ThunderBolt into the USB dock. As as alternate solution, I plugged the U2415’s DisplayPort directly into the MBP as a second input and was able to use both displays after that.
