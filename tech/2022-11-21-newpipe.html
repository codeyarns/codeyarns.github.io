NewPipe is a privacy-focused Youtube video app for Android. It is useful to users who want to watch videos, avoid ads, keep their own list of channels/subscriptions without giving that data to Google. It is similar in intention to the FreeTube app for Windows/Linux.

  • Source: In addition to Youtube, this app also supports other similar sources such as SoundCloud, BandCamp and a few others no one uses. Press the ‚ò∞ and choose the source you want from the dropdown under the NewPipe name.

  • üî• Trending: This shows a random list of trending videos from Youtube. Not sure how this list is generated and I have never used this.

  • üì∫ Subscriptions: This lists all the individual channels you are subscribed to and all the **channel groups* you have created from those channels. (In NewPipe source code, a channel is called a subscription and a channel group is called a feed group.)

    • All the channels you manually (or imported) subscribed to are collected under the All channel group here.
    • I find it useful to create other channel groups (like comedy, national news, local news etc.) and add the already-subscribed channels to a channel group.
    • Clicking on a channel group shows the last videos from that channel group. Pull down to refresh the videos from that channel group.

  • ‚ñ∂ What‚Äôs New: This shows a combined list of all new videos from all the channels you are subscribed to. This is essentially what the All channel group in Subscriptions shows too.

  • üîñ Bookmarked Playlists: This is empty until you start adding individual videos to playlists that you create. The first time you to try to add a video it will ask you to create a playlist with a new name.

    • I like to add videos to a To Watch list and catch up on those videos under this section.
    • I also like to have a playlist of all the live TV stream videos I like to watch.

  • Video playback options: When a video is playing there are options at the top that allow you to change the playback speed and switch to different chapter (if chapters are present in the video). At the bottom of the app window, you can switch between comments, similar videos and video info (from left to right).

  • Import Youtube channels/subscriptions: Due to the privacy-focused design of the app, there is no direct importing of your Google/Youtube account in this application. Instead follow these steps:

    • Go to Google Takeout and export your Youtube subscriptions. Google will make it available to you as a CSV file.
    • Go to Subscriptions in the NewPipe app, choose ‚ãÆ at the top-right corner and choose Import from -> Youtube. Import the CSV file you had obtained from Google Takeout.
    • Once imported, you will find all the recent videos from your channels listed under Subscriptions under the All channel group.

  • Import/Export from NewPipe:

    • Subscriptions: Use the import/export option in the top-right corner in the subscriptions tab. This imports/exports just the list of channels (not the channel groups) into a JSON file.
    • Settings: Use the import/export option in the settings section. This imports/exports channels, channel groups, playlists and all other settings into a .zip file. This file has a .db file (SQLite database) and a .settings file (Java serialized data).
    • Sync: Either of the above settings can be imported/exported between NewPipe instances on different devices. However, there is no way to sync. Importing will overwrite existing settings.

  • Sync to FreeTube: I use FreeTube on Windows for the same reason I use NewPipe on Android. It would have been convenient to sync my subscriptions and channel groups between Android and Windows. Sadly there does not seem to be a way to do this.

  • Android TV: Though NewPipe supports Android TV officially, it is a difficult experience to use it. I was able to install the app easily using F-Droid. However, the UX and controls have not been adapted for non-touch use and are a pain to use with remote.

    • Video cannot be made to play in full screen by default and the keyboard isn‚Äôt enabled wherever text needs to be entered forcing me to use the onscreen keyboard for text entry.
    • To get from the seeking to full-screen button, you need to press down on the remote, not right.
    • Importing subscriptions or settings from file does not work. The file open dialog doesn‚Äôt show any of the files from the TV filesystem or that from an inserted USB flashdrive.

  • Plugins: Though NewPipe itself does not have any plugins, folks have created forks of it with SponsorBlock and other plugins here.
