NewPipe is a privacy-focused Youtube video app for Android. It is useful to users who want to watch videos, avoid ads, keep their own list of channels/subscriptions without giving that data to Google. It is similar in intention to the FreeTube app for Windows/Linux.

  • Source: In addition to Youtube, this app also supports other similar sources such as SoundCloud, BandCamp and a few others no one uses. Press the â˜° and choose the source you want from the dropdown under the NewPipe name.

All the channels you manually (or imported) subscribed to are collected under the All channel group here. I find it useful to create other channel groups (like comedy, national news, local news etc.) and add the already-subscribed channels to a channel group. Clicking on the channel group shows the latest videos from that channel group.

  • â–¶ Whatâ€™s New: This shows a combined list of all new videos from all the channels you are subscribed to. This is essentially what the All channel group in Subscriptions shows too.

  • Video playback options: When a video is playing there are options at the top that allow you to change the playback speed and switch to different chapter (if chapters are present in the video). At the bottom of the app window, you can switch between comments, similar videos and video info (from left to right).

  • Import Youtube channels/subscriptions: Due to the privacy-focused design of the app, there is no direct importing of your Google/Youtube account in this application. Instead follow these steps:

    • Once imported, you will find all the recent videos from your channels listed under Subscriptions under the All channel group.
