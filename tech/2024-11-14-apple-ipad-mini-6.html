The Apple iPad Mini (6th generation) went on sale in September 2021 and I got it in November 2024. I decided to get it after my experiment with a small tablet as ebook reader worked out well and I decided to upgrade to an iPad Mini in the Starlight color.

Specifications

  • iPadOS: 17.5.1 (21F90)
  • Model number: MK7P3LL/A
  • Buttons
    • Top left: Vol up/down
    • Top right: Power / Touch ID
  • Connectivity: USB-C at bottom
  • Bluetooth: 5.0
  • Wifi: 6 802.11ax
  • SoC: A15 Bionic 2.92 GHz (6-core CPU, 5-core GPU, 16-core Neural Engine)
  • Display: 8.3-inch 2266x1488 Liquid Retina (326 PPI)
  • Front camera: 12 MP
  • Rear camera: 12 MP

User Interface

  • Home Screen: Similar to desktop on computers, the main central region of display where the app icons are shown. Swipe up from the bottom edge of screen to get the home screen. It can have multiple pages, swipe left or right to navigate the pages.

  • App Library: Swipe left past all the pages to get this. Shows all the installed apps organized into categories.

  • Dock: Swipe up from the bottom edge only a little bit to reveal the Dock at the bottom. It shows the most recently used apps, the right-most button is the App Library.

  • App Switcher: Swipe up from the bottom edge, stop in the center of the display and hold to get the App Switcher. Swipe up on an app to quit it.

  • Control center: Shown when you swipe down from top-left part of display. Provides quick access to common settings like wifi, bluetooth, brightness and rotation.

  • Split view: Feature used to display 2 apps side by side. Only available on iPads.

  • Lock Screen: Press and hold on the lock screen to get the customize option. Create and customize your own lock screen here.

  • Widgets: Like app buttons, but can be larger and show information from inside an app directly on the Home Screen.

  • Folder: Drag one app on another to open up a new Folder that holds both.

Usage

  • Search: Swipe down from the middle of the screen to get search. Works both from Home Screen or the Lock Screen.

  • Use as second display for Macbook: Having the iPad Mini as a second display (sidecar) is useful not just as additional screen estate for windows, but to use the Apple Pencil (or similar writing device) to jot notes, annotate or draw through iPad in any Macbook app that supports it. First, make sure both the Macbook and iPad are signed into the same Apple account and both the devices are visible in Devices section of the account in both of them. Next, make sure the devices are near each other and on the same wifi network. Connecting the iPad with USB-C cable to the Macbook is a better solution. Finally, choose the iPad in the Screen Mirroring option on the Macbook and further in the Displays settings you can choose if you want to mirror or extend the display to the iPad.
